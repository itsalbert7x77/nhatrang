## What Is a Professional Dental Cleaning?

**A professional dental cleaning removes plaque, tartar, and surface stains that regular brushing cannot eliminate. Regular dental cleanings are the foundation of good oral health. Even with diligent brushing and flossing, plaque hardens into tartar within 24-48 hours in areas that are difficult to reach. Tartar can only be removed by a dental professional. Left untreated, it leads to gum disease, cavities, and eventually tooth loss.

### What the Cleaning Includes

1. **Examination** — Your dentist checks for cavities, gum disease, and other issues.
2. **Ultrasonic scaling** — High-frequency vibrations break apart tartar deposits above and below the gumline.
3. **Hand scaling** — Fine instruments remove remaining deposits in tight spaces.
4. **Polishing** — A rotating cup with prophylaxis paste smooths tooth surfaces and removes stains.
5. **Fluoride treatment** (optional) — A protective fluoride varnish is applied to strengthen enamel.
6. **Oral hygiene advice** — Personalised tips for maintaining healthy teeth at home.

### Recovery Tips

- Mild gum sensitivity or bleeding is normal for a day or two after cleaning, especially if there was significant tartar.
- Avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes if fluoride treatment was applied.
- Use a soft toothbrush and brush gently for the first day.
- Continue regular flossing — the spaces between teeth may feel different now that tartar has been removed.
- Schedule your next cleaning in 6 months to maintain the results.
