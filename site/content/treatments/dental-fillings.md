### Types of Fillings Available

- **Composite resin** (from $8) — Tooth-coloured material that bonds directly to the tooth. The most popular choice for visible teeth.
- **Glass ionomer** (from $8) — Releases fluoride to help prevent further decay. Often used near the gumline.
- **Ceramic inlay/onlay** ($50-$100) — Lab-fabricated porcelain restorations for larger cavities. More durable and aesthetic than direct fillings.
- **Amalgam** (from $5) — Silver-coloured metal filling. Extremely durable but less aesthetic. Rarely requested now.

### Recovery Tips

- Wait until the local anaesthesia wears off before eating to avoid biting your cheek or tongue.
- Mild sensitivity to hot and cold is normal for 1-2 weeks after a filling, especially deeper ones.
- Avoid very hard or sticky foods on the new filling for 24 hours.
- If the bite feels uneven, return to the clinic for a quick adjustment — this is free and takes minutes.
- Continue normal brushing and flossing to prevent new cavities.
