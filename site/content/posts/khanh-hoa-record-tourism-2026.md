Khanh Hoa province — home to the coastal gem of Nha Trang — is making headlines in 2026 with record-breaking tourism numbers. For dental tourists considering Vietnam, this surge in visitors signals better infrastructure, more flight options, and improved services across the board.

## The Numbers Behind the Boom

The early months of 2026 have been extraordinary for Khanh Hoa. Cam Ranh International Airport handled over 512 takeoffs and landings during the New Year holiday alone, a 4% increase from 2025. International flights accounted for 321 of those, with passenger volumes jumping 15% year-over-year.

The province is targeting over 18.1 million visitors and 77,000 billion VND in tourism revenue for 2026 — ambitious figures that reflect the region's growing appeal to travelers from South Korea, Russia, China, and Southeast Asia.

## What This Means for Dental Tourists

The tourism boom is a net positive for anyone planning dental work in Nha Trang:

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **More flights** | New direct routes from Singapore, Phnom Penh, and expanded connections from Seoul and Moscow |
| **Better hotels** | Over 90% occupancy has pushed hotels to upgrade services and compete on quality |
| **Improved infrastructure** | Roads, transport links, and tourist facilities are being upgraded to handle demand |
| **Clinic investment** | Dental clinics are investing in modern equipment and multilingual staff to serve international patients |

## Peak Season Tips for Dental Tourists

With occupancy rates exceeding 90% during holidays, planning ahead is essential. Book your dental consultation and hotel at least three weeks before arrival. Many clinics now offer package coordination with nearby hotels, making it easy to combine treatment with a beach holiday.

The shift toward luxury tourism also means more premium accommodation options near dental clinics. Patients recovering from implant procedures or cosmetic work can now choose from a wider range of comfortable, high-end stays.

## A City Ready for International Patients

Nha Trang's rise in global tourism rankings — climbing to 31st in the Global Cities Attractiveness Index — reflects real improvements in the visitor experience. For dental tourists, this translates to English and Korean-speaking clinic staff, better airport transfers, and a wider choice of restaurants and activities during recovery days.

The province's focus on quality over quantity means that dental clinics are under pressure to maintain international standards, which benefits patients seeking reliable, affordable care.
