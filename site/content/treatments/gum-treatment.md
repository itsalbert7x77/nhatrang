## What Is Gum Treatment?

**Gum treatment addresses periodontal disease — infection and inflammation of the gums and supporting bone structures around the teeth. Gum disease is one of the most common dental conditions and the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. It develops when plaque and tartar accumulate below the gumline, causing inflammation, deep periodontal pockets, and gradual bone loss. Early treatment is essential to prevent irreversible damage.

### Types of Gum Treatment Available

- **Scaling and root planing** ($40-$80 per quadrant) — Deep cleaning below the gumline to remove tartar and smooth root surfaces, allowing gums to reattach.
- **Laser gum therapy** ($80-$150 per quadrant) — Minimally invasive laser treatment that removes infected tissue and promotes regeneration.
- **Flap surgery** ($100-$150 per quadrant) — The gums are lifted to clean deep pockets and reshape bone before repositioning.
- **Gum grafting** ($120-$180 per area) — Tissue is grafted to cover exposed roots caused by gum recession.

### Recovery Tips

- Gums may be sore and swollen for 2-5 days after deep cleaning or surgery. Use prescribed pain medication.
- Rinse gently with warm salt water or prescribed antimicrobial mouthwash 2-3 times daily.
- Brush gently with a soft toothbrush, avoiding vigorous scrubbing on treated areas.
- Avoid hard, crunchy, and spicy foods for the first few days.
- Do not smoke — smoking significantly impairs gum healing and increases risk of treatment failure.
- Take all prescribed antibiotics as directed, completing the full course.
