**Dental braces use brackets and wires to gradually move teeth into proper alignment, correcting crooked teeth, overcrowding, and bite problems. ### Types of Braces Available

- **Metal braces** ($400-$800) — Traditional stainless steel brackets. The most affordable and effective option for all case types.
- **Ceramic braces** ($600-$1,200) — Tooth-coloured brackets that blend with your teeth. Less visible but slightly more fragile.
- **Self-ligating braces** ($800-$1,800) — Use a sliding mechanism instead of elastic ties. Faster adjustments and potentially shorter treatment times.
- **Lingual braces** ($1,200-$1,800) — Placed behind the teeth for invisible treatment. More complex and requires specialised training.

### Recovery Tips

- Expect soreness for 3-5 days after placement and each adjustment. Soft foods and pain relievers help.
- Avoid hard foods (nuts, hard candy, ice) and sticky foods (caramel, chewing gum) that can break brackets.
- Brush after every meal using an orthodontic brush. Use floss threaders or a water flosser.
- Use orthodontic wax on brackets that irritate the inside of your cheeks.
- Keep all adjustment appointments on schedule to avoid extending treatment time.
